# Common Network Security Threats

## Introduction

In today's digital world, computer networks are used everywhere, from schools and colleges to businesses and government organizations. As technology grows, cyber threats are also increasing. These threats can affect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information. Therefore, it is important to understand common network security threats and the methods used to prevent them.

## 1. Denial of Service (DoS) Attack

A Denial of Service (DoS) attack is a cyberattack in which an attacker attempts to make a website, server, or network unavailable to legitimate users. This is usually done by sending a large amount of traffic to the target system.

When the server receives more requests than it can handle, it becomes slow or completely stops responding. As a result, genuine users cannot access the service.

### Impact

* Website or server downtime
* Loss of business operations
* Financial losses
* Reduced customer trust

### Real-World Example

One famous example is the 2016 attack on Dyn, a major DNS provider. The attack affected popular websites such as Twitter, Netflix, and Reddit, making them inaccessible to many users.

### Prevention Methods

* Using firewalls
* Implementing traffic filtering
* Load balancing
* Monitoring network traffic regularly

---

## 2. Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attack

A Man-in-the-Middle attack occurs when an attacker secretly intercepts communication between two parties. The attacker can listen to the communication, steal information, or even modify the data being exchanged.

This type of attack often happens on unsecured public Wi-Fi networks where users connect without proper protection.

### Impact

* Theft of usernames and passwords
* Financial fraud
* Leakage of sensitive information
* Unauthorized access to accounts

### Real-World Example

Attackers sometimes create fake public Wi-Fi hotspots in crowded places such as airports and cafes. Users who connect to these networks may unknowingly expose their personal information.

### Prevention Methods

* Use secure HTTPS websites
* Avoid connecting to unknown Wi-Fi networks
* Use a Virtual Private Network (VPN)
* Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

---

## 3. Spoofing Attacks

Spoofing is a technique where an attacker pretends to be a trusted person, device, or organization. The goal is to trick users into believing the communication is legitimate.

Common types of spoofing include:

* Email Spoofing
* IP Spoofing
* ARP Spoofing
* DNS Spoofing

For example, an attacker may send an email that appears to come from a bank or a trusted company to steal sensitive information.

### Impact

* Identity theft
* Data breaches
* Malware infections
* Unauthorized system access

### Real-World Example

Many phishing emails use email spoofing to imitate trusted companies and persuade users to click malicious links.

### Prevention Methods

* Verify sender information carefully
* Use email security filters
* Keep software updated
* Educate users about cybersecurity risks

---

## Conclusion

Network security threats such as DoS attacks, Man-in-the-Middle attacks, and spoofing can cause serious damage to individuals and organizations. Understanding how these attacks work is the first step toward protecting systems and data. By following security best practices, using protective technologies, and staying aware of cyber risks, organizations can significantly reduce the chances of becoming victims of cyberattacks.
